Research Focus: Machine learning for geometric accuracy improvement in additive manufacturing.

Effective Registration of 3D Scanned Point Clouds for Accuracy Assessment of AM Parts

Unrestricted registration of 3D scanned point clouds of 3D printed parts to CAD models can lead to alignments that underestimate a part's geometric deviations.  This line of research illustrates the magnitude of this bias, and provides a recommended methodology for minimizing these issues.

Shape Deviation Modeling for the Improvement of 3D Print Accuracy

Dimensional deviations between a 3D printed part and its intended shape can lead to costly scrap and rework.  This line of research demonstrates a method for quantifying, predicting and prescriptively compensating for a part's errors before they happen using past print data from different shapes and machine learning.

Geometric Accuracy Prediction for Additive Manufacturing Through Machine Learning of Triangular Mesh Data (Proceedings) (Researchgate)

A Simplified Benchmarking Model for the Assessment of Dimensional Accuracy in FDM Processes (Journal) (Researchgate)

Use of Post-Consumer Thermoplastics in 3D Printing

The low rate of recycling for thermoplastics is a serious cause for concern, while 3D printing offers a unique opportunity for destributed recycling.  This line of research demonstrates the potential of binary blends of recycled high-density polyethylene and acrylonitrile butadiene styrene as feedstock for 3D printers.

Assessing the Use of Binary Blends of Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene and Post-Consumer High Density Polyethylene in Fused Filament Fabrication (Journal) (Researchgate)
