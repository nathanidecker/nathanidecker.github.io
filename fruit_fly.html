Mechatronic Device for Monitoring Drosophila

In 2016, Nate Deffenbaugh and I began a research project with the Department of Biological Sciences at Biola University. Our goal was to develop a mechatronic device that would induce and monitor sleep deprivation in Drosophila Melanogaster. Our final product uses a four bar linkage system to rotate the fruit fly enclosures in multiple directions in order to maintain fly activity. We also developed a computationally inexpensive algorithm to quantify fruit fly activity levels in real-time via a single HD video input and MATLAB. Finally, a MATLAB GUI controls motion scheduling, data capturing/display/output, and day/night cycles with built in lights.
